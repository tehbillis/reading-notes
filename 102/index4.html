1. Gather Supplies

Make sure you have all the necessary supplies before you start, including the correct oil filter, new engine oil of the recommended viscosity, an oil pan to catch the old oil, wrenches for removing the drain plug and oil filter, and a jack and jack stands to lift the car.

2. Prepare the Vehicle

  • Park your car on a level surface to ensure accurate oil drainage and safety.
  • Set the parking brake to prevent the car from rolling while you work.
  • Lift the front of the car using a jack and secure it with jack stands for safety.

3. Drain Old Oil

  • Locate the oil drain plug underneath the engine. Consult your owner's manual for its exact location.
  • Position the oil pan beneath the drain plug to catch the old oil.
  • Use a wrench to carefully loosen the drain plug, then remove it by hand.
  • Allow the old oil to drain completely into the oil pan.

4. Replace Oil Filter

  • PLocate the old oil filter, usually near the oil pan. Refer to your owner's manual if needed.
  • Place an oil pan beneath the oil filter to catch any dripping oil.
  • Use an oil filter wrench to carefully loosen and remove the old filter.
  • Before installing the new filter, apply a thin layer of clean oil to the rubber gasket on the top of the filter.
  • Screw on the new oil filter by hand until snug, then give it a quarter-turn to secure it.

5. Add New Oil

  • Replace the drain plug and tighten it securely.
  • Open the oil fill cap on top of the engine.
  • Use a funnel to pour in the recommended amount of new engine oil. Refer to your owner's manual for the correct oil type and capacity.
  • Allow a few moments for the oil to settle before checking the level using the dipstick.

6. Dispose of Old Oil

Carefully transfer the old oil from the oil pan to a sealed container that can be properly disposed of at a recycling center or automotive shop.

7. Lower the Vehicle

Carefully lower the car from the jack stands using the jack.

8. Run the Engine

  • Start the engine and let it run for a few minutes to circulate the new oil throughout the system.
  • While the engine is running, check around the drain plug and oil filter for any leaks. If you notice any, promptly address them.

9. Check Oil Level

  • Turn off the engine and wait a few minutes for the oil to settle in the oil pan.
  • Use the dipstick to check the oil level. Add more oil if necessary to bring it to the recommended level.

10. Clean Up

  • Wipe off any spilled oil from the engine and undercarriage using a rag.
  • Properly store your tools and dispose of the used materials, including the old oil and oil filter, at an appropriate recycling or disposal facility.
